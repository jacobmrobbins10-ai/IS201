The Season Nobody Expected

On July 31, 2021 the Atlanta Braves sat at 44-45, a game below .500, and half a game out of last place in the NL East. Injuries to Ronald Acuna Jr and others plagued their season. They were written off as an afterthought. Then everything changed.

Over the final two months of the season, the Braves went 44-22, won the NL East, battled through the playoffs and took down the Houston Astros in 6 Games to claim the franchise's first title since 1995.

This is the story of that run

The Postseason Run

Atlanta played three series, each with its defining moments, in order to raise the trophy.

  1. NLDS vs. Milwaukee Brewers - Won 3-1

    The Braves took down the Brewers, who had the best record in the NL, in four games. Key moments included:

    • Joc Pederson's Game Three Home Run
    • Charlie Morton's Dominant Game One Start
    • Freddie Freeman's Go-Ahead Homer in the Bottom of the 8th off Josh Hader in Game 4

  2. NLCS vs. Los Angeles Dodgers - Won 4-2

    The Dodgers were the defending World Series Champions, and the heavy favorites in this series.

    • Eddie Rosario named NLCS MVP (.560 avg, 3 HR, 9 RBI). He set the Braves Franchise Record for most hits in a single postseason series with 17.
    • Back to back walk off game winners in Games 1 and 2 to go up 2-0.
    • Tyler Matzek throws the best inning Braves history en route to a Game 6 clinch in Atlanta.

  3. The World Series vs. The Houston Astros - Won 4-2

    The World Series. The pinnacle of baseball. Against one of the most hated teams in baseball.

    • Jorge Soler's iconic Game 6 HR to seal the title
    • Max Fried's Epic Game 6 Performance
    • Jorge Soler and Dansby Swanson go back to back late in a brutally cold Game 3

Unsung Heroes

Atlant's title was built on contributions from players all over the field. They needed everybody to play their best.

RF

Jorge Soler

Acquired at the trade deadline from Kansas City, Soler hit 3 Home Runs in the World Series, and was named World Series MVP. His Game 6 Home Run ended up being the dagger.

LF

Eddie Rosario

Traded from the Cleveland Indians, Rosario hit .560 in the NLCS, one of the best playoff performances of the last several decades.

SP

Max Fried

He delivered one of his best performances in Game 6. Avoiding an early ankle scare, he held the Astros scoreless through 6 innings.

SS

Dansby Swanson

Swanson became a hometown hero for his home runs in the series, and was a key part of the final out in both the NLCS and the WS.
